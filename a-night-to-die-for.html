    During my sophomore year at Parsons, my Advanced Typography class was given an exciting project—to create a visual identity for an event from our imagination. This ad campaign required a series of posters, an invitation, and wayfinding signage. For my event, I decided to use a murder mystery dinner party, which I titled A Night To Die For, or ANTDF.

    My inspiration behind the visual identity was a series of advertisement posters from the 1930s, as well as archetypes from the film noir genre. Using a custom font, I typeset the letters in a slightly chaotic, crooked manner in order to portray the sinister theme. I looked at examples of telegraphs for the design of the invitation.
